# karon-residences

> **Note:** This is a proof of concept driven by two personal goals: building a platform that runs my short-term rental side business with minimal human intervention, and learning how to leverage AI across the full product development lifecycle — from ideation to rollout.

## Learn from my mistakes

[rose.karon.se/blog](https://rose.karon.se/blog/)

---

Most short-term rental hosts don't realize they're building someone else's business. Every booking made through Airbnb or Booking.com costs 15–20% in commission, but the deeper loss is structural: the host owns nothing — not the guest relationship, not the data, not the brand, not the rules. When the platform changes its algorithm, raises its fees, or decides to promote competitors, the host has no alternative and no leverage. For a property owner running two or three well-placed apartments, this dependency isn't a minor inconvenience — it's a permanent ceiling on margin, autonomy, and long-term value.

This platform is the exit ramp. It's a branded direct booking website that lets guests discover, book, and pay without any intermediary taking a cut. Built for a small portfolio starting with properties in Stockholm and Mil Palmeras on Spain's Costa Blanca, it gives the owner a real channel they control — their own pricing logic, cancellation policies, guest communication, and brand identity. Direct bookings mean higher margins and, over time, a repeat guest base that doesn't need to be reacquired through an OTA every single stay.

What makes this different from just "having a website" is the operational layer underneath. The platform is designed to run with minimal human input, powered by modular AI agents that handle the full guest lifecycle automatically. Before a booking, agents are writing SEO content and running targeted social ad campaigns to drive discovery. During the booking process, they handle instant confirmation and payment. Throughout the stay, they manage guest messaging, check-in instructions, and FAQ responses in English, Swedish, German, and Dutch. After checkout, they send follow-up messages and review prompts. A small team of three supervises and approves — but the machine runs on its own. This isn't automation as a cost-cutting measure; it's automation as the core business model.

The timing is right for exactly this kind of platform. Direct booking as a category is maturing — guests are increasingly comfortable booking outside of OTAs, especially for properties with a clear brand and professional presentation. AI tooling has reached a level of reliability where agentic workflows are genuinely viable for small operations, not just enterprise. And a portfolio starting at one to three properties is the right scale to build and validate the system before expanding. The goal isn't to manage ten properties manually — it's to prove that the agentic infrastructure works at small scale, then grow the portfolio on top of it.
